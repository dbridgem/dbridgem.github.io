About Me

     I'm currently a Doctor of Chemical Engineering working at a small startup in the Phoenix area. I earned my Bachelor's and Doctorate of Philosophy in Chemical Engineering at Arizona State University, then took a position at TF Health Corporation developing sensors to guide health-related lifestyle decisions. The flagship product of TF Health Co. is the Breezing Tracker, which is a breath-based device that utilizes indirect calorimetry to measure metabolism.

     I started at ASU in 2009 getting my undergraduate, and started actual lab work in 2012 as a volunteer. After a few months I was offered a position, which is when I started working a fair bit with data in Matlab. After I graduated, I started as a Research Assistant in my PhD.. During my PhD. I worked on several projects, with my dissertation being on developing an inexpensive carbon dioxide colorimetric sensor. This project was mostly building a model to account for temperature, moisture, and transport delays in a sensor system where none of these things could actually be measured all that well by reference methods.

     After I graduated, I started working at TF Health Corporation, where I worked to develop the second generation of their flagship product. This is when I started to branch out: At this point I'm proficient with Python, SQL, Matlab, R, HTML/CSS, C, and others. This is also when I started to apply more machine learning techniques. At this point I am proficient with most common machine learning techniques: linear regression, logistic regression, decision trees / random forests, support vector machines and regressions, artificial neural nets, nearest neighbor classification (KNN), and many more.

     I am a capable data scientist looking for my next challenge. For more detail on some related projects see the project page above or take a look at my resume. Thanks for stopping by.
